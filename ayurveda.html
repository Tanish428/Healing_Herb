What is Ayurveda ?

Ayurveda is a holistic system of medicine that originated in India thousands of years ago.The word Ayurveda is made from two Sanskrit roots "Ayu" which mean life and "Veda" which means knowledge. Therefore, the term Ayurveda means the knowledge or science of life.



1.Five Elements (Pancha Mahabhutas):

  • Earth (Prithvi) : Represents stability and structure.

  • Water (Jala) : Represents fluidity and cohesion.

  • Fire (Agni) : Represents transformation and energy.

  • Air (Vayu) : Represents movement and communication.

  • Ether (Akasha) : Represents space and consciousness.


2. The Three Doshas:

  • Vata (Air + Ether) : Governs movement, including breathing, circulation, and nerve impulses. It is associated with qualities like dryness, cold, lightness, and irregularity.

  • Pitta (Fire + Water) :Governs transformation and digestion. It is associated with heat, sharpness, intensity, and metabolism.

  • Kapha (Earth + Water): Governs structure and lubrication. It is associated with heaviness, stability, moisture, and coldness.


3. Herbal Healing Mechanism:

  • Active Compounds: Represents stability and structure.

  • Balance Doshas : Represents fluidity and cohesion.


Common Herbs and Their Uses:

  • Ginger :Aids digestion, relieves nausea, and reduces inflammation.

  • Turmeric :Anti-inflammatory, helps with joint pain, digestive issues, and skin conditions.

  • Garlic :Supports heart health, reduces cholesterol, and fights infections.

  • Tulsi (Holy Basil): Eases respiratory issues, reduces stress, and boosts immunity.

  • Ashwagandha : Reduces stress and anxiety, supports energy levels, and boosts immunity.

  • Neem :Antibacterial, antifungal, and helps with skin conditions like acne and eczema.

  • Chamomile :Calms the nervous system, aids sleep, and helps with digestive issues.

  • Peppermint :Relieves digestive discomfort, headaches, and muscle pain.
